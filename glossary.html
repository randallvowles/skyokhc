Wikipedia Meteorology Pages
Atmospheric Convection Atmospheric convection is the result of a parcel-environment instability, or temperature difference layer in the atmosphere.
Clouds A cloud is an aerosol consisting of a visible mass of minute liquid droplets, frozen crystals, or other particles suspended in the atmosphere.
Geographical Zones The five main latitude regions of the Earth's surface.
Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ) The Intertropical Convergence Zone is the area where the northeast and southeast trade winds converge
Lake-effect Snow Lake-effect snow is produced during cooler conditions when a cold air mass moves across long expanses of warmer lake water.
Orographic Lift Orographic lift occurs when an air mass is forced from a low elevation to a higher elevation as it moves over rising terrain.
Precipitation Precipitation is any product of the condensation of atmospheric water vapour that falls under gravity.
Squall Lines A squall line or quasi-linear convective system (QLCS) is a line of thunderstorms forming along or ahead of a cold front.
Tornadoes A tornado is a rapidly rotating column of air that is in contact with both the surface of the Earth and a cumulonimbus cloud.
Waterspout A waterspout is an intense columnar vortex (usually appearing as a funnel-shaped cloud) that occurs over a body of water.
Weather Fronts A weather front is a boundary separating two masses of air of different densities.
